Momo

The sweet peachy Syrian Hamster




Momo was an adorable Syrian Hamster. She loved to take sand baths, dig her cage floor and eat cheese.

She was rescued in Pedroso, Vila Nova de Gaia, and lived her whole life in Lisbon. She also travelled to Algarve, making her a super tourist rodent.

She had a boyfriend - Pompom - who she enjoyed to bite and fight with (and would love to kill him too). They had 7 babies back in March 2018, and 2 of them lived with her: Hoshi and Tama.

Sadly, Momo passed away in 2019. She was very old, but I believe she lived a happy life, filled with love and cheese treats.

Syrian Hamsters (or Golden Hamsters) are originate from Syria and were first described and officially named in 1839, by George Robert Waterhouse, a British zoologist.

Waterhouse's original hamster was a female named Cricetus Auratus, meaning "golden hamster". The skin of the animal is kept at the Natural History Museum in London. (like, ew, c'mon!)
